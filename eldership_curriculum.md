## I. Foundational Standards & Documents

These documents form the constitutional backbone of the curriculum. Every elder candidate must know them thoroughly.

1. **Westminster Confession of Faith** — with the footnote on the Pope and Antichrist retained, and the footnote ensuring the three calls of power can be called (irregular magistrate, officers without a settled court, ordinary delegated settled court)
2. **Westminster Larger Catechism**
3. **Westminster Shorter Catechism**
4. **Form of Government** — modified version requiring the teaching office (two-office clarification) and specifying the three votes of the congregation
5. **Directory of Worship** — including the Q&A section and the section on anointing with oil and laying on of hands for the sick
6. **Directory of Family Worship** — with clarifications to prevent misinterpretation in a tyrannical or oppressive manner

### Historical Focus Areas (in priority order)

1. **Bible History** — Genesis through 70 AD (the most important)
2. **Major Councils** — Nicaea, Chalcedon, Orange
3. **The Persecuted Church** — pre-Constantinian era
4. **Papal Dominion** — c. 600 AD to the Reformation
5. **Reformation History** — through the "Killing Times"
6. **Second Reformation** — John Knox and the Covenanter testimonies
7. **Decline of American Presbyterianism** — the drift into non-establishmentarianism and the unscriptural 1789 alterations
8. **Preterist & Postmillennial documents** — for thinking about the current church era

### Chapter 5: Of Providence

Largely covered by the decree materials above. Key concepts to master:

- **Second causes and causation** — covered in Clark's works
- **Hobbes and mechanism** — be aware of this error
- **Leibniz and dualism** — the "two clocks" metaphor (spiritual world / material world)
- **Four metaphysical positions:** Dualism, Materialism, Idealism (Spiritualism), and the Biblical view (Creation — the spiritual causes the physical by God creating)

**Other systematic theologies — strengths and cautions:**
